Hallo! - Learn to say hello and bye
Hallo, Hi

"Hallo" is the most common greeting in German and can be used in almost any situation.

"Hi" is a more casual version of "Hallo".

 Hello, Hi
Tschüss!

“Tschüss” is an informal way to say goodbye.

 Bye!
Introducing yourself - Learn how to say your name
Ich heiße Anna.

We can spell it "heiße" or "heisse". The ß stands for a double s.

 My name is Anna.
Ich bin Felix.

We can say "ich heiße" or "ich bin" to introduce ourselves.

 I'm Felix.
Freut mich!

The "eu" in "Freut mich" is pronounced similar to “oi” in “boy”.

“Freut mich” literally translated means “it makes me happy to get to know you”.

 Nice to meet you!
Asking for a name - Learn new phrases for introductions
Ich heiße Ben. Wie heißt du?

"du" means: you (singular).

 My name is Ben. What's your name?
Wie heißt du?

“Wie heißt du?” is used among friends and in informal conversations.

The “ß” in “heißt” is not a b, it’s pronounced like a double “ss”.

 What's your name?
Freut mich auch!

You can reply to "Freut mich" by saying "Freut mich auch".

 Nice to meet you, too!

"Hallo"

"Hallo" (hello) is a common greeting you can use with anyone.
Wie heißt du?

To find out someone's name, we ask: "Wie heißt du?"

 What's your name?

We can say "ich heiße" or "ich bin" to introduce ourselves. We can say "hallo" or "hi" to greet someone.
Ich heiße Christiane. Wie heißt du?

"Ich" means: I. "Du" means: you (singular informal).

 I'm Christiane. What's your name?
Freut mich!

The phrase "Nice to meet you" consists of "Freut" and "mich" (literally "pleases me").

 Nice to meet you!

"Freut mich auch" literally means: "Pleases me too".

When you introduce yourself to someone, it is polite to follow up your name with “Freut mich!“.

I'm Anna. Nice to meet you!

"Hallo! Ich heiße... Freut mich!" is how you can introduce yourself in German.

Hello! My name is Miriam. Nice to meet you!
